Getting Started

How do I know if therapy or coaching is right for me?

If you're feeling stuck, struggling in your relationships, disconnected from your body, or simply knowing there's more to life than what you're currently experiencing — that's often a sign that support could help. I offer a free consultation where we can talk about what you're going through and whether my approach is a good fit.

What happens in a free consultation?

The consultation is a brief 15-20 minute call where you can share what brings you to therapy or coaching, ask any questions, and get a feel for what it would be like to work together. There's no pressure and no obligation.

Do you offer virtual or in-person sessions?

Both! I offer sessions via secure video platform as well as in-person at my office. We'll work together to find the format that feels most comfortable and effective for you.

About Therapy

What is the difference between therapy and coaching?

Therapy typically addresses mental health conditions, explores past experiences and their impact on the present, and involves diagnosis and treatment. Coaching is more future-focused and action-oriented, helping you build skills, set goals, and create the life you want. Both can be powerful — and during our consultation, I can help you determine which is the best fit for your needs.

How long does therapy typically last?

This varies widely depending on your goals, what you're working on, and how deeply you want to go. Some clients find what they need in a few months; others benefit from longer-term work. We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust as needed.

Is everything I share confidential?

Yes. Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. What you share stays between us, with very few exceptions required by law (such as imminent danger to yourself or others, or mandated reporting of abuse). I'll go over these limits in detail during our first session so you know exactly what to expect.

About Somatic Sex Therapy

What is somatic sex therapy?

Somatic sex therapy is a body-based therapeutic approach that addresses sexual concerns, intimacy challenges, and embodiment through direct experience rather than talk alone. It incorporates breath work, movement, sensation awareness, and mindful touch practices to help you reconnect with your body and its natural capacity for pleasure and healing.

Is somatic sex therapy sexual?

Somatic sex therapy is a professional, therapeutic modality — it is not a sexual service. While the work addresses sexuality and the body, sessions are always boundaried, consent-driven, and conducted within a clear therapeutic framework. The goal is healing and growth, supported by a trained professional in a safe environment.

What does a somatic sex therapy session look like?

Sessions typically begin with a check-in and then move into experiential exercises. These might include breath work, body scanning, guided movement, or awareness practices. Everything is offered as an invitation — you are always in control of what you participate in. We go at your pace, always.

Logistics & Policies

Do you accept insurance?

I am an out-of-network provider. I can provide you with a superbill (detailed receipt) that you can submit to your insurance company for potential reimbursement. Many PPO plans offer out-of-network benefits. I recommend contacting your insurance provider to ask about your out-of-network mental health coverage.

What is your cancellation policy?

I ask for at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations or missed sessions may be subject to the full session fee. I understand that life happens, and we can discuss any extenuating circumstances.

Do you offer sliding scale rates?

Yes. I believe this work should be accessible to those who need it. I have a limited number of sliding scale spots available. Please feel free to ask during your consultation, and we can discuss what works for your situation.
